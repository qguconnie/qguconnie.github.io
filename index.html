What is HydroFlake

An innovative Tangible User Interface (TUI) system merging hydration monitoring with creative engagement that enhances creativity.

Why HydroFlake

We want to address the issue of inadequate hydration and creativity among office workers.

Why do we design HydroFlake ?

Hydration is crucial for overall well-being and productivity. Efforts have been made to develop social tools for cultivating hydration habits. Research has shown that tangible creative support tools can enhance productivity and creativity. Additionally, research highlights the effectiveness of learning through tangible materials and gamification in fostering creative thinking habits. Our project extends this research by integrating tangible and playful creativity into the cultivation of healthy hydration habits. We also explore the social impact of hydration behavior cultivation and its connection to accountable productivity.

What is the problem ?

Despite the recognized importance of hydration for overall well-being, many people struggle to maintain adequate water intake throughout the day, especially people who work in office settings. Additionally, maintaining creativity during busy work days is challenging in fast-paced workplaces. Existing hydration tracking methods often lack creativity and fail to provide ongoing motivation and accountability. Our project aims to create a solution that merges hydration tracking with tangible creative play to empower individuals to prioritize their well-being while nurturing their creativity.

HydroFlake is our solution that...

introduces an approach to tracking hydration while fostering creativity. Featuring up to 16 dynamically shaped coasters — reflecting the average daily water intake of 15.5 cups for men and 11.5 cups for women—the system transforms into a desktop ornament as users reach their hydration goals. Equipped with water level sensors, the coasters dynamically adjust their hue based on the water level. When a cup is emptied, signaling a hydration achievement, the coaster shifts from colorless to vibrant color. Once a cup coaster changes color, users replace it with a new one under the cup and assemble the colored pieces together. This design encourages both hydration and daily creativity through the construction of unique structures.


We prototyped with little bits and experimented with different materials for cup coasters, including cardboard boxes and 3D printing


Design and Materials

We chose acrylic as the material for our coasters due to its durability and transparency, which allows the LED colors to show through. The shapes were designed using Adobe Illustrator and cut by laser cutters.



Technical Aspects

We utilized a rainwater level sensor to monitor the remaining liquid in the cup. To secure the sensor to the mug, we 3D printed a holder resembling a teabag. Using an Arduino board, we programmed it to illuminate different colored lights corresponding to various water levels. We started with three individual LEDs connected in parallel. Later, we transitioned to using an RGB LED, reducing the number of LEDs in the coaster to one. The system is programmed so that the light is colorless when the water is full, green when the water level is low, and red when it's medium.

Final System

Our final system consists of a set of 16 coasters, with a water level sensor and a box that stores the coasters and the arduino board. Find our complete code at our GitHub repo.
